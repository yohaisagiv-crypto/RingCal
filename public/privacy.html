🔵 RingCal — Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

RingCal ("we", "the app") is a personal calendar application. This policy explains what data is collected and how it is used.

1. Data Storage

All calendar events, tasks, categories, and settings are stored locally on your device only (in your browser's IndexedDB or the app's local storage). We do not operate any servers that collect or store your personal data.

2. Google Services (Optional)

If you choose to connect Google Calendar or Google Drive, the app uses the following Google API scopes:

  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/calendar — to read and write calendar events
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/drive.file — to store a backup file in your Google Drive

These connections are entirely optional. OAuth tokens are stored locally on your device and are never transmitted to our servers. Your Google data is accessed only to provide the sync and backup features you explicitly enable.

3. AI Features (Optional)

The AI assistant feature uses Google Gemini. If you choose to use it, you provide your own personal API key, which is stored locally on your device. Calendar data sent to Gemini is used solely to generate AI responses and is subject to Google's AI terms.

4. Notifications

If you enable notifications, the app schedules local notifications on your device. No notification data is transmitted to external servers.

5. Data We Do Not Collect

We do not collect, transmit, or sell any personal data. There are no analytics, tracking pixels, or third-party advertising SDKs in this app.

6. Data Deletion

To delete all app data: in your browser, clear site data for this app; on Android, go to Settings → Apps → RingCal → Clear Data.

7. Children

This app is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children.
